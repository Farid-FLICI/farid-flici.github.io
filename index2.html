Research

I am an actuarial demographer working at the intersection of mortality modelling, health inequalities, and pension sustainability. My research combines statistical modelling and policy-oriented analysis to study population ageing, migrant health, and social protection systems across Algeria, Europe, and the UK.

CV Publications Projects Software

Migrant Mortality & Health Inequalities

My recent research examines mortality and health inequalities between migrants and native populations in high-income countries. I analyse how country of origin shapes mortality advantages and how these patterns evolve over time.

This work relies on large-scale administrative data and contributes to ongoing debates on migrant health and demographic change in Europe and the UK.

View Paper Project

Population Ageing & Pension Sustainability

I study the financial sustainability of pension systems under population ageing, with a focus on PAYG schemes. My work explores reform options such as contribution caps and demographic risk management strategies.

I also develop simulation tools to assess long-term system balance under alternative policy scenarios.

Health Expectancy

My research on health expectancy examines how increases in longevity interact with chronic disease prevalence. I develop models combining mortality and morbidity to estimate healthy life expectancy and its future evolution.

Mortality Evolution in Algeria

A core part of my work focuses on mortality dynamics in Algeria, including life table construction, mortality forecasting, and long-term demographic trends.
