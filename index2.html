About

I am a Senior Research Fellow at the Research Center in Applied Economics for Development (CREAD-Algiers), PhD in Applied Statistics (ENSSEA, 2017), Specialized in mortality modeling, migrant mortality and health inequalities, population aging, and pension sustainability. My Research bridges actuarial science, demography, and public policy to address demographic challenges in Algeria and high-income countries. I am Accredited local actuary, consultant for a number of United Nations Agencies, and designated member of many policy advising bodies.

Research Interests

  • Migrant mortality and health inequalities in high-income countries
  • Population aging and retirement system sustainability
  • Data science for population studies
  • Chronic disease impact on healthy life expectancy
