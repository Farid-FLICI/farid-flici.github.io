About

I am a Senior Research Fellow at CREAD (Algiers) and Visiting Researcher at the London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine (LSHTM), specializing in actuarial demography. I hold a PhD in Statistics (ENSSEA) with a background in actuarial science, and I have a strong interest in data science and its applications to population studies. I am a nationally accredited actuary, with extensive experience applying actuarial and demographic methods to real-world policy challenges. My work lies at the intersection of mortality modelling, health inequalities, population ageing, and the financial sustainability of pension and social protection systems. I collaborate widely with governmental institutions in Algeria, including the Ministries of Health, Finance, Labour and Social Security, National Solidarity, and the National Statistical Authority. I have also worked with international organisations and UN agencies both in Algeria and abroad, contributing to projects related to population dynamics, health, and social protection. Beyond research, I am actively involved in global initiatives aimed at strengthening actuarial education in low- and middle-income countries, notably through the Actuarial Faculty Development Program (AFDP) and the Global Actuarial Initiative (GAIN). My academic work includes publications in peer-reviewed international journals and leadership of several research projects as Principal Investigator, in Algeria, Spain, and the United Kingdom. Through this work, I aim to contribute to evidence-based policymaking and to advance the integration of actuarial science, demography, and public policy.
