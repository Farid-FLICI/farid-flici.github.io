I'm an actuary with a PhD in statistics. I am a Senior Research Fellow at the Research Centre in Applied Economics for Development - CREAD in Algiers (Algeria), where I investigate mortality and longevity, ageing, and pension sustainability. I was the previous director of the Social Economics Research Department, the team leader for population research, and the founder of the Actuarial Demography Working Group. I am currently a Visiting Researcher at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine (Population Studies Group), where I am working on the mortality advantage of migrants from MENA countries in the UK. In addition, I hold a variety of advisory and consulting roles. I am a member-expert on the Algerian health ministry's national population committee, an expert on the National Council of Statistics, the president of the social statistics commission, an advisor on the National Economic, Social, and Environmental Council, and an actuary accredited by the Algerian Union of Insurance and Reinsurance Companies. I also work as an Individual Consultant for the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA) and the United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Western Asia (UNESCWA). On the other hand, I volunteer as a mentor for "R for Actuaries" and "Pensions" in the Actuarial Faculty Development Program (AFDP), which is part of the Global Actuarial Initiative (GAIN), a project launched by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), Milliman, and ACTEX to promote actuarial sciences in developing countries.
